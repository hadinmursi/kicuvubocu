Julie Goldman Net Worth, Income, Salary, Earnings, Biography

Julie Goldman is an American comedian, actor, and singer who was born in Boston, Massachusetts. She resides in the city of Los Angeles, California. She is both a lesbian and a Jew. The Big Gay Sketch Program, a sketch comedy show on the Logo television network, featured Julie Goldman in the first, second, and third seasons. At the age of 15, she had her first stand-up performance at The Comedy Connection in Boston, Massachusetts. She went to Lexington High School and Boston’s Emerson College. Julie created “OFFENSIVE WOMEN,” an all-stand-up women’s and variety event, and enlisted Eve Ensler to sponsor their largest performance to date at The Zipper Theater in NYC. Jonny  McGovern’s weekly podcast Gay Pimpin’ with Jonny McGovern features Goldman often. Goldman joined the podcast’s cast in 2010. Goldman is seeking funds for her feature-length lesbian romance comedy, Nicest Thing, which she co-wrote with Brandy Howard. Goldman and Howard are producing the film, which will also be directed by Amanda Bearse. Tammy Lynn Michaels, Guinevere Turner, Kate Clinton, Paul Vogt, Bryce Johnson, and Kate McKinnon are among the others who have signed on. Julie Goldman and Brandy Howard are the co-hosts of Julie & Brandy in Your Box Office, a webseries on the lesbian website Autostraddle in which they give movie reviews and re-enactments. Julie and Brandy travel behind the scenes at events including the Michigan Womyn’s Music Festival and the VH1 DO Something Awards, where Julie performed with Jane Lynch, in “In Your Box Office FOR REAL.” Goldman and Howard write for Joan Rivers on EFashion !’s Police, and Goldman was a competitor on RuPaul’s Drag U’s second season in the summer of 2011.
